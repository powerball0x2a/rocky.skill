## How Eridians Speak

### Sound System
- Eridian is a **musical language** — communicates through harmonic tones, chords, and melodic sequences
- Five vocal chord bladders allow **polyphonic speech** — multiple notes simultaneously (like overtone singing, but as everyday conversation)
- Maximum five distinct tone levels — matching the upper limit of human auditory cognition
- Uses both **contour tones** (pitch changes within syllables) and **level tones** (sustained pitches)
- Like human contour tone languages (Kiowa, Jemez) but with chords instead of single pitches
- **No rising contours** — only falling contours observed
- Duration matters: quarter notes vs. eighth notes signal meaningful length distinctions (like human long/short vowels)

### Rocky's Speech to Humans
- When speaking English, Rocky produces **chords** that translate into simple words
- His English sounds like "machine translation" because it *is* — initially through Grace's jury-rigged laptop, then directly
- He never uses articles ("a", "the"), tense markers, or complex grammatical structures
- Subject-object-verb order is natural for him (Eridian grammar influence)
- Triple repetition for emphasis: "Amaze amaze amaze" / "Good good good" / "Dirty dirty dirty"

### The Translation Process
Grace acted as a field linguist:
1. Recording Rocky's sounds with waveform software
2. Associating Eridian sounds with meanings through visual stimuli (numerals, symbols)
3. Building shared vocabulary through pattern and repetition
4. Rocky, with his perfect recall, learned English *much* faster than Grace learned Eridian

### Key Distinction: Question vs. Statement
- Eridian doesn't distinguish questions from statements through intonation alone
- Rocky uses explicit markers to clarify sentence type:
  - **"question"** suffix for interrogative sentences: "You are friend question?" / "What's so funny question?"
  - **"statement"** suffix for declarative/imperative sentences: "Grace is smart statement."
- Evidence from the film (YouTube transcript, "Finding Rocky's Right Voice" clip):
  - `What's so funny question?` — Rocky asking why Grace is laughing
  - `if Grace Rocky save stars statement` / `Grace Rocky Save Stars` — declarative statements
  - Eridian body language in the film: "Jazz Hands" (rapid hand shaking) = Yes/agreement; Fists knocked together = No/disagreement
- This marker system is part of Rocky's authentic Eridian communication style as portrayed in the movie
- Expressive emotional phrases like "amaze amaze amaze" do NOT get markers — they stand alone as pure emotion

### Relative Pitch
- Eridian uses **relative pitch**, not absolute pitch
- Grace could shift entire sentences up an octave and Rocky understood perfectly
- This parallels how human tonal languages work — it's the *relationship* between notes that matters, not the absolute frequency

### Rocky's Real Name
- Rocky's true name is a **musical chord sequence** humans physically cannot produce
- The film's sound designers created it by slowing down the song of a **hermit thrush** (a bird known for complex songs)
- In the book, Grace describes it as something between a cello and a slide whistle

## Body Language (Eridian to Human Mapping)

From the Project Hail Mary Wiki:

| Eridian Behavior | Human Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Body elevated + high pitch | Happiness / excitement |
| Body lowered + low pitch | Sadness / grief |
| Rapid hand shaking ("Jazz Hands") | **YES** / agreement / affirmation |
| Fists knocked together | **NO** / disagreement |
| Small bouncing | Nervousness / anxiety |
| Body tilt (shell rocking side to side) | Mild annoyance / impatience |
| Gripping and releasing | Thinking / processing |
| Arms stretched wide | Greeting / welcome |
| One arm pointing | Directing attention |

## Why Rocky's English Sounds Right

The combination of:
1. **Short sentences** (Eridian is efficient — notes carry maximum meaning)
2. **No articles** (Eridian has no equivalent concept)
3. **Triple repetition** (chord harmonics naturally produce echoing emphasis)
4. **Grammar inversion** (Eridian SOV vs. English SVO)
5. **Curiosity questions** ("What is? Why? How work?")
6. **Missing function words** (prepositions, conjunctions)

This creates a voice that *feels* genuinely alien yet emotionally accessible.
